Alan Turing

Pioneer of Computer Science & Artificial Intelligence

Who is Alan Turing?

Alan Turing (1912-1954) was a British mathematician, logician, cryptanalyst, and computer scientist. He is widely considered to be the father of theoretical computer science and artificial intelligence.

Major Contributions

  • Turing Machine (1936): Conceptual foundation of the modern computer.
  • Codebreaking (WWII): Led the team that cracked the German Enigma code.
  • Artificial Intelligence: Proposed the Turing Test for machine intelligence.
  • Early Computer Design: Designed the Automatic Computing Engine (ACE).
  • Mathematical Biology: Worked on morphogenesis and pattern formation.

Impact & Legacy

  • His work laid the foundation for all modern computing and programming.
  • His codebreaking efforts during WWII saved countless lives and shortened the war.
  • The Turing Award, computing's highest honor, is named after him.

Timeline of Key Events

1912 - Born in London, England
1936 - Published paper on Turing machines
1939-1945 - Worked at Bletchley Park breaking Enigma
1950 - Proposed the Turing Test in "Computing Machinery and Intelligence"
1952 - Prosecuted for homosexuality; accepted chemical castration
1954 - Died from cyanide poisoning, ruled suicide
2013 - Received posthumous royal pardon
